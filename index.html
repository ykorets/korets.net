The Foundation

How it started

Three career chapters, a war, three countries. One thread through all of it: build from scratch, build to last.

Journalism & Politics · 2000-2008

Started as a culture journalist in Donetsk. The 2004 Orange Revolution pulled me to Kyiv, where I became editor of the politics section. Then a year as press secretary in the Verkhovna Rada, building a committee's press office from zero. I saw the state machine from inside and decided politics wasn't my game. Moved into commercial PR, then left to start my own thing.

Ebola Communications · 2008-2015

Built a digital agency from solo operation to a team of 15. Sold it to Atlantik Group, a major ad holding, before turning 30. No mentors, no MBA. After the deal, I led the merged entity, Liquid7 Production, managing nearly 100 people and 30 simultaneous clients: Coca-Cola, McDonald's, Carlsberg, Pepsi, Credit Agricole, Raiffeisen Bank. In the final year, we won the EFFIE Award for Most Effective Digital Agency in Ukraine. That felt like the right ending to the chapter.

Exploration & Reinvention · 2015-2023

Invested everything from the agency sale into Pecker Player, an OTT streaming box. Built a strong product, but the 2014 war in Donbas collapsed the market. Got an offer to launch in Russia. Refused on principle. Product shut down. Then came a 2,000-square-meter community space in Kyiv (closed by COVID), VP of Real Estate Development at a construction holding in Odessa (stopped by the 2022 war), COO at an IT holding (left when my growth strategy was rejected). Each stop taught me something. The IT holding rejected my strategy, then ChatGPT launched a year later and broke their business.

KPI Creatives · 2023-Now

Started in LA as a video production marketplace (IndieV). Found an investor, launched, then pivoted to B2B when the marketplace model couldn't compete. Now it's a Creative Growth Partner with a team of 15, building content systems for US businesses in construction, real estate, and entertainment. The model is clear: earn trust before the sale, deliver results after. Scale through AI and systems, not headcount.

Burning Man · Since 2013

First burn in 2013. Founded the Ukrainian Burners Community and NGO in Ukraine. Now President of Ukrainian Burners Inc., a 501(c)(3) in California. I run Kurenivka Camp, the first and biggest official Ukrainian camp at Burning Man: 5 official burns, 6+ art projects brought to playa, 500+ total participants. Founded the Blue Bull art car based on Maria Prymachenko's naive art, with 20+ team members, 30+ artists, and a partnership with the Prymachenko Family Foundation. In 2025, Blue Bull rolled into the first circle facing the Man on Burn Night. We hosted Benny Benassi and Be Svendsen. I also DJ and throw BBQs. This isn't a hobby. It's a live test of leadership, logistics, and trust under extreme conditions.

Philosophy

How I Think

01

Content is infrastructure. Not marketing collateral, not a campaign. A system you build once and compound over time.

02

Small teams with AI beat large headcount. I've managed nearly 100 people. I know when fewer is more.

03

If you can't be the decision-maker, don't take the job. I left a C-level role over this. The strategy I proposed was rejected. A year later, ChatGPT proved it right.

04

Teams matter more than ideas. Community matters more than teams. Nothing meaningful is built alone.

05

War changes your priorities permanently. My son was born during bombardment of Odessa. We relocated through three countries. You stop caring about small things after that.
